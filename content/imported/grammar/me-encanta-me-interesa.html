How to Use “Me encanta” and “Me interesa” in Spanish

Me encanta / me interesa - I love it, I'm interested

Simple grammar: Talking about what we like

The verbs Encantar and Interesar work differently in Spanish

How to use gustar in Spanish:

1) The verb changes depending on what you like:

  • Me encanta el chocolate → I like chocolate
  • Me interesa ese libro → I'm interested in that book. (You like ONE thing )
  • Me encantan los chocolates → I like chocolates
  • Me interesan esos libros → I'm interested in those books (You like MORE THAN ONE thing)

2) Use "interesar" o “encantar” with verbs:

  • Me interesa comer sano → I'm interested in eating healthy
  • Me encanta bailar → I love to dance

Use “encantan” only with plural nouns:

  • Me encantan los chocolates → I like chocolates

Oraciones afirmativas

  • Me encanta la música clásica. – I love classical music.
  • Te interesan los museos. – You are interested in museums.
  • Le encanta correr en el parque. – He/She loves running in the park.
  • Nos interesa aprender idiomas. – We are interested in learning languages.
  • Les encantan las películas de terror. – They love horror movies.

Oraciones negativas

  • No me interesa el fútbol. - I am not interested in soccer.
  • No te encanta el café. - You don't love coffee.
  • A ella no le interesan los videojuegos. - She is not interested in video games.
  • No nos encanta la lluvia. - We don't love the rain.
  • A ellos no les interesa la literatura antigua. - They are not interested in ancient literature.

Oraciones interrogativas

  • ¿Te encanta el chocolate? - Do you love chocolate?
  • ¿Le interesa la política? - Does he/she interest in politics? (o más común: Is he/she interested in politics?)
  • ¿Nos encantan los animales? - Do we love animals?
  • ¿Les interesa el arte moderno? - Are they interested in modern art?
  • ¿Me interesa este curso? - Am I interested in this course?
