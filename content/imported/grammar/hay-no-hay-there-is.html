How to use HAY / NO HAY in Spanish

“HAY” means “there is” or “there are.”
“NO HAY” means “there is not” or “there are not.”

1) Use HAY to say what there is or isn’t:

Hay + sustantivo (noun)

  • Hay un gato. → There is a cat.
  • Hay dos gatos. → There are two cats.
  • Hay una mesa en la cocina. → There is a table in the kitchen.

No hay + sustantivo (noun)

  • No hay leche. → There is no milk.
  • No hay clases hoy. → There are no classes today.
  • No hay estudiantes en la sala. → There are no students in the room.

Oraciones afirmativas - Affirmative sentences

  • Hay un perro en el parque. – There is a dog in the park.
  • Hay tres sillas en el comedor. – There are three chairs in the dining room.
  • Hay una fiesta esta noche. – There is a party tonight.
  • Hay flores en el jardín. – There are flowers in the garden.
  • Hay agua en la botella. – There is water in the bottle.

Oraciones negativas - Negative sentences

  • No hay pan en la casa. – There is no bread in the house.
  • No hay estudiantes en la clase. – There are no students in the class.
  • No hay tiempo. – There is no time.
  • No hay luz. – There is no light.
  • No hay autobuses hoy. – There are no buses today.

Oraciones interrogativas

  • ¿Hay un baño aquí? – Is there a bathroom here?
  • ¿Hay comida en la nevera? – Is there food in the fridge?
  • ¿Hay una farmacia cerca? – Is there a pharmacy nearby?
  • ¿Hay turistas en la ciudad? – Are there tourists in the city?

Expresiones comunes con “hay”

No hay problema. → No problem!
