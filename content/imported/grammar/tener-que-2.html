How to Use “Tener que” in Spanish

Tener que – Presente

"Tener que" means have to.
It's used to express obligation.

Conjugación

Tener que is followed by an infinitive verb (unconjugated).

  • Yo tengo que trabajar. – I have to work.
  • Yo tengo que estudiar. – I have to study.

Oraciones afirmativas - Affirmative sentences

  • Yo tengo que comer vegetales. - I have to eat vegetables.
  • Nosotros tenemos que trabajar. - We have to work.

Oraciones negativas - Negative sentences

  • No tengo que estudiar hoy. - I don't have to study today
  • No tengo que limpiar la casa. - I don't have to clean the house

Oraciones interrogativas - Interrogative sentences

  • ¿Tienes que trabajar mañana? - Do you have to work tomorrow?
  • ¿Nosotros tenemos que cocinar la cena? - Do we have to cook dinner?

Ejercicios de traducción

  • I have to study for the exam. → __________________
  • He has to clean the room. → __________________
  • We have to buy food. → __________________
  • You (plural) have to walk to school. → __________________
  • They have to go to the supermarket. → __________________

Responder

¿Tú tienes que limpiar tu habitación? - Do you have to clean your room?

¿Tú tienes que almorzar? – Do you have to eat lunch?

¿Tú tienes que estudiar hoy? – Do you have to study today?

¿Tú tienes hermanos? – Do you have siblings?

¿Tú tienes un carro? – Do you have a car?
