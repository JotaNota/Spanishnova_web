“Ir a” + Infinitive in Spanish

Uso de “ir a” + infinitivo

In Spanish, we use “ir a” + infinitive to talk about things we are going to do in the future.

t’s similar to “going to” in English and expresses plans or intentions.

Explicación

Structure

The structure is: [conjugación de ir] + a + [verbo en infinitivo]

  • Yo voy a estudiar – I’m going to study

Cómo usar “ir a” + infinitivo

  1. Conjugate the verb "ir" according to the subject; e.g with “yo”: Yo voy.
  2. Add the preposition “a”. → Yo voy a
  3. Use the main verb in the infinitive (unconjugated). → Yo voy a estudiar

Ejemplo: Yo voy a estudiar español. → I am going to study Spanish.

It is used to express intentions, decisions, or actions that will happen soon.

Conjugación

Oraciones afirmativas

  • Yo voy a estudiar español. — I am going to study Spanish.
  • Tú vas a cocinar la cena. — You are going to cook dinner.
  • Ella va a leer un libro. — She is going to read a book.
  • Nosotros vamos a ir al cine. — We are going to go to the cinema.
  • Ellos van a visitar a sus abuelos. — They are going to visit their grandparents.

Oraciones negativas

  • Yo no voy a salir hoy. — I am not going to go out today.
  • Tú no vas a comer pizza. — You are not going to eat pizza.
  • Él no va a estudiar inglés. — He is not going to study English.
  • Nosotros no vamos a trabajar mañana. — We are not going to work tomorrow.
  • Ellos no van a viajar este mes. — They are not going to travel this month.

Oraciones interrogativas

  • ¿Vas a venir conmigo? — Are you going to come with me?
  • ¿Ella va a estudiar medicina? — Is she going to study medicine?
  • ¿Nosotros vamos a cenar juntos? — Are we going to have dinner together?
  • ¿Van a viajar mañana? — Are they going to travel tomorrow?
  • ¿Qué vas a hacer hoy? — What are you going to do today?
  • ¿Dónde van a vivir? — Where are they going to live?
