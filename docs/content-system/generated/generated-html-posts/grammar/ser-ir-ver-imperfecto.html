Ser, ir, and ver are the three everyday irregular verbs in the imperfect. Use them to describe what someone was like, where someone used to go, and what someone was seeing or watching.

Overview

The forms are era, iba, and veía. They are short, common, and useful for real past descriptions.

  • Cuando era niño, iba al parque. → When I was a child, I used to go to the park.
  • Desde la ventana, veía el río. → From the window, I could see the river.

Cómo/cuándo lo usamos

Ser for descriptions

Use ser in the imperfect for identity and descriptions in the past.

  • Mi escuela era pequeña. → My school was small.
  • Ellos eran vecinos. → They were neighbors.

Ir and ver for repeated scenes

Use ir for repeated movement and ver for what someone saw or watched.

  • Íbamos al cine los viernes. → We used to go to the movies on Fridays.
  • Veíamos las noticias por la noche. → We used to watch the news at night.

Estructura

Patrón Example Translation
subject + era/eras/éramos + description La clase era interesante. The class was interesting.
subject + iba/ibas/íbamos + a + place Íbamos al mercado los sábados. We used to go to the market on Saturdays.
subject + veía/veías/veíamos + object Veíamos películas en casa. We used to watch movies at home.

Oraciones.

Afirmativas

  • Yo trabajaba en el centro. → I used to work downtown.
  • Tú comías con tu familia. → You used to eat with your family.
  • Ella vivía cerca. → She used to live nearby.
  • Nosotros íbamos al parque. → We used to go to the park.

Negativas

  • Yo no salía mucho. → I did not use to go out much.
  • Él no tenía coche. → He did not have a car.
  • Nosotras no veíamos televisión. → We did not use to watch TV.
  • Ellos no eran vecinos. → They were not neighbors.

Forma de pregunta

  • ¿Trabajabas los sábados? → Did you use to work on Saturdays?
  • ¿Ella vivía aquí? → Did she use to live here?
  • ¿Íbamos juntos? → Did we use to go together?
  • ¿Ustedes veían esa serie? → Did you all use to watch that series?
