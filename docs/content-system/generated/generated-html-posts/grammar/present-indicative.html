The present indicative describes actions, habits, and facts in the present. Regular Spanish verbs use different endings depending on whether the infinitive ends in -ar, -er, or -ir.

Overview

Use the present indicative to talk about what happens now, what happens regularly, and what is generally true.

Yo estudio español todos los días. → I study Spanish every day.
Use: present actions, routines, and facts

Uses

Current actions and routines

Use the present indicative for actions happening now and routines that happen regularly.

  • Yo preparo la cena ahora. - I am preparing dinner now.
  • Caminamos al trabajo todos los días. - We walk to work every day.

Facts and general statements

Use the present indicative for facts, descriptions, and general statements.

  • El restaurante abre a las nueve. - The restaurant opens at nine.
  • Mis amigos viven en Bogotá. - My friends live in Bogotá.

Oraciones

Afirmativa

  • Yo hablo con mi profesora. - I talk with my teacher.
  • Tú comes fruta por la mañana. - You eat fruit in the morning.
  • Ella vive cerca de la escuela. - She lives near the school.
  • Nosotros estudiamos español juntos. - We study Spanish together.

Negativa

  • Yo no trabajo los domingos. - I do not work on Sundays.
  • Tú no bebes café por la noche. - You do not drink coffee at night.
  • Él no vive en el centro. - He does not live downtown.
  • Ellas no estudian en la biblioteca. - They do not study at the library.

Preguntas

  • ¿Hablas español en casa? - Do you speak Spanish at home?
  • ¿Comen ustedes juntos? - Do you all eat together?
  • ¿Dónde vive tu hermana? - Where does your sister live?
  • ¿Trabajan ellos por la tarde? - Do they work in the afternoon?
