# Tener in the Present Tense

The verb **tener** means **to have**. In the present tense, use it to talk about possession, age, family, needs, and common everyday expressions.

## Overview

**Tener** is an irregular verb in the present tense. The **yo** form is **tengo**, and some forms use **ie**: **tienes**, **tiene**, and **tienen**.

- Yo tengo un cuaderno. — I have a notebook.

## Structure

| Structure | Example | Translation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| subject + tener + noun | Ella tiene una bicicleta. | She has a bicycle. |
| subject + no + tener + noun | Nosotros no tenemos dinero. | We do not have money. |
| ¿subject + tener + noun? | ¿Tú tienes tiempo? | Do you have time? |

## Conjugation

| Subject | Form | Example | Translation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Yo | tengo | Yo tengo una pregunta. | I have a question. |
| Tú | tienes | Tú tienes un perro. | You have a dog. |
| Él / Ella / Usted | tiene | Ella tiene dos hermanos. | She has two siblings. |
| Nosotros / Nosotras | tenemos | Nosotros tenemos una clase. | We have a class. |
| Ellos / Ellas / Ustedes | tienen | Ellos tienen tarea. | They have homework. |

## Uses

### Possession

Use **tener** to say that someone has a thing, a person, or an object.

- Tengo una mochila. — I have a backpack.
- Ustedes tienen libros. — You all have books.

### Age And Common Needs

Use **tener** for age in Spanish and with common expressions like **tener hambre** or **tener sueño**.

- Mi hermana tiene diez años. — My sister is ten years old.
- Tenemos hambre. — We are hungry.

## Oraciones

### Afirmativa

- Yo tengo una casa. — I have a house.
- Tú tienes una familia grande. — You have a big family.
- Ella tiene un trabajo nuevo. — She has a new job.
- Nosotros tenemos tiempo hoy. — We have time today.

### Negativa

- Yo no tengo dinero. — I do not have money.
- Tú no tienes tarea. — You do not have homework.
- Él no tiene un carro. — He does not have a car.
- Ellas no tienen clase mañana. — They do not have class tomorrow.

### Preguntas

- ¿Tienes un lápiz? — Do you have a pencil?
- ¿Ella tiene hermanos? — Does she have siblings?
- ¿Ustedes tienen tiempo? — Do you all have time?
- ¿Tenemos una reunión hoy? — Do we have a meeting today?
