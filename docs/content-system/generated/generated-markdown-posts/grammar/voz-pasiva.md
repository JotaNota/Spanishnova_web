## Overview

The passive voice focuses on what receives the action instead of who does it. Spanish often uses ser plus participle for formal passive sentences, and se for more natural passive-like statements. Focus on the message first, then choose the form that fits the context.

- Passive voice: ejemplo principal. -> Passive voice: main example.
- Passive voice: otro ejemplo ?til. -> Passive voice: another useful example.

## Cómo/cuándo lo usamos

### Real use

Use this pattern when passive voice helps express a real message, not just a grammar label.

### Common context

This form often appears in everyday explanations, plans, advice, and reactions depending on the sentence.

## Estructura

| Patrón | Example | Translation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| trigger + que + verb form | Passive voice: quiero que uses la forma. | I want you to use the form. |
| si + condition, result | Passive voice: si cambia el contexto, cambia la forma. | If the context changes, the form changes. |
| reported/passive pattern | Passive voice: la idea se expresa con claridad. | The idea is expressed clearly. |
