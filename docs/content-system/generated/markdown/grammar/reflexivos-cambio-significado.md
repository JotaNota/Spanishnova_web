Some verbs change meaning when they become reflexive. The reflexive version often describes a personal change, a result, or the subject's involvement in the action.

## Overview

Compare the non-reflexive verb with the reflexive form. Ir means to go, but irse means to leave. Dormir means to sleep, but dormirse means to fall asleep.

- Voy al banco. -> I go to the bank.
- Me voy a las ocho. -> I leave at eight.

## Cómo/cuándo lo usamos

### Movement versus leaving

Use ir for going to a place and irse for leaving a place or situation.

- Ellos van a la escuela. -> They go to school.
- Ellos se van después de la clase. -> They leave after class.

### Action versus result

Some reflexive forms focus on the result or change for the subject.

- Leo en la cama. -> I read in bed.
- Me duermo en la cama. -> I fall asleep in bed.

## Estructura

| Patrón | Example | Translation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ir | Vamos al centro. | We go downtown. |
| irse | Nos vamos ahora. | We are leaving now. |
| dormir / dormirse | El niño se duerme temprano. | The child falls asleep early. |

## Oraciones.

### Afirmativas

- Voy a la oficina. -> I go to the office.
- Me voy de la oficina. -> I leave the office.
- Ella duerme ocho horas. -> She sleeps eight hours.
- Ella se duerme en el sofá. -> She falls asleep on the sofa.

### Negativas

- No me voy todavía. -> I am not leaving yet.
- Él no se duerme en clase. -> He does not fall asleep in class.
- Nosotros no nos quedamos aquí. -> We do not stay here.
- Ustedes no se llevan el libro. -> You all do not take the book with you.

### Forma de pregunta

- ¿Te vas ahora? -> Are you leaving now?
- ¿Ella se duerme rápido? -> Does she fall asleep quickly?
- ¿Nos llevamos las llaves? -> Do we take the keys with us?
- ¿Ellos se quedan en casa? -> Do they stay at home?
