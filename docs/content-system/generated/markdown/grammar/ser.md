  <h1>Easy Grammar: Ser in the Present Tense</h1>
  <p class="intro">The verb <strong>ser</strong> means <strong>to be</strong>. Use it for identity, origin, professions, relationships, descriptions, and personality traits.</p>
</section>

<section class="panel" id="overview">
  <h2>How do we use ser?</h2>
  <p>Use <strong>ser</strong> when you talk about what someone or something is in a basic or defining way.</p>

  <div class="simple-example">
    <div class="example-line">
      <strong>Yo soy Ana.</strong>
      <span class="example-arrow">-></span>
      <em>I am Ana.</em>
    </div>
    <div class="use-line"><span class="use-label">Use:</span> identity and names</div>
  </div>

  <p>Spanish verbs change form depending on the subject. This change is called conjugation.</p>
</section>

<section class="panel" id="uses">
  <h2>Common uses of ser</h2>
  <div class="two-column">
    <div class="example-group">
      <h3>Identity and names</h3>
      <ul class="sentence-list">
        <li><strong>Yo soy Marco.</strong> <span class="translation">I am Marco.</span></li>
        <li><strong>Ella es Sofía.</strong> <span class="translation">She is Sofía.</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div class="example-group">
      <h3>Origin</h3>
      <ul class="sentence-list">
        <li><strong>Él es mexicano.</strong> <span class="translation">He is Mexican.</span></li>
        <li><strong>Nosotras somos chilenas.</strong> <span class="translation">We are Chilean.</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div class="example-group">
      <h3>Professions</h3>
      <ul class="sentence-list">
        <li><strong>Ella es ingeniera.</strong> <span class="translation">She is an engineer.</span></li>
        <li><strong>Tú eres profesor.</strong> <span class="translation">You are a teacher.</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div class="example-group">
      <h3>Descriptions</h3>
      <ul class="sentence-list">
        <li><strong>Él es alto.</strong> <span class="translation">He is tall.</span></li>
        <li><strong>Ustedes son amables.</strong> <span class="translation">You all are kind.</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="panel" id="examples">
  <h2>Examples with ser</h2>

  <div class="grammar-block">
    <h2>Affirmative sentences</h2>
    <div class="example-bubble">
      <ul class="sentence-list">
        <li><strong>Yo soy estudiante.</strong><br><span class="translation">I am a student.</span></li>
        <li><strong>Tú eres mi hermano.</strong><br><span class="translation">You are my brother.</span></li>
        <li><strong>Ella es de Colombia.</strong><br><span class="translation">She is from Colombia.</span></li>
        <li><strong>Nosotros somos amigos.</strong><br><span class="translation">We are friends.</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="grammar-block">
    <h2>Negative sentences</h2>
    <p>Add <strong>no</strong> before the verb.</p>
    <div class="example-bubble">
      <ul class="sentence-list">
        <li><strong>Yo no soy médico.</strong><br><span class="translation">I am not a doctor.</span></li>
        <li><strong>Él no es argentino.</strong><br><span class="translation">He is not Argentinian.</span></li>
        <li><strong>Ellas no son profesoras.</strong><br><span class="translation">They are not teachers.</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="grammar-block">
    <h2>Yes/no questions</h2>
    <p>Use question marks and a question voice.</p>
    <div class="example-bubble">
      <ul class="question-list">
        <li><strong>¿Tú eres Ana?</strong><br><span class="translation">Are you Ana?</span></li>
        <li><strong>¿Él es tu amigo?</strong><br><span class="translation">Is he your friend?</span></li>
        <li><strong>¿Ustedes son estudiantes?</strong><br><span class="translation">Are you all students?</span></li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>
