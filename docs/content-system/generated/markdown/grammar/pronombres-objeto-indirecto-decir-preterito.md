Decir in the preterite is common with indirect object pronouns because we often tell something to someone. Use me, te, le, nos, and les before dije, dijiste, dijo, dijimos, and dijeron.

## Overview

The structure is simple: indirect object pronoun + decir in the preterite + what was said. Le dije la verdad means I told him or her the truth.

- Le dije la verdad. -> I told him the truth.
- Nos dijeron la hora. -> They told us the time.

## Cómo/cuándo lo usamos

### Tell information to someone

Use this pattern when the person receives information, news, an answer, or a warning.

- Te dije la dirección. -> I told you the address.
- Nos dijeron la noticia. -> They told us the news.

### Use a + person for clarity

Add a mí, a ti, a él, a ella, or a ellos when le or les could be unclear.

- Le dije a Ana que no. -> I told Ana no.
- Les dije a mis amigos la verdad. -> I told my friends the truth.

## Estructura

| Patrón | Example | Translation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| indirect object pronoun + verb + thing | Le mando un mensaje. | I send him a message. |
| no + indirect object pronoun + verb | No me respondió. | She did not answer me. |
| indirect object pronoun + verb + a + person | Le escribo a Ana. | I write to Ana. |

## Oraciones.

### Afirmativas

- Le escribo a Ana. -> I write to Ana.
- Me dieron una respuesta. -> They gave me an answer.
- Nos mandaron el archivo. -> They sent us the file.
- Les expliqué el problema. -> I explained the problem to them.

### Negativas

- No le dije nada. -> I did not tell him anything.
- Ella no me respondió. -> She did not answer me.
- No nos enviaron la dirección. -> They did not send us the address.
- No les gustó la idea. -> They did not like the idea.

### Forma de pregunta

- ¿Me mandaste el enlace? -> Did you send me the link?
- ¿Le escribiste a tu jefe? -> Did you write to your boss?
- ¿Nos explican el plan? -> Do they explain the plan to us?
- ¿Les diste el número? -> Did you give them the number?
