# Conversation Structure Variants - SpanishNova

Use `default_variant` from `docs/content-system/content-plan/conversation-roadmap.csv` to choose the lesson format.

The roadmap stores only the selector. Format, voice, rhythm, and section behavior live here.

## podcast_style

Use for lesson entries that feel like a short Spanish learning podcast episode.

Editorial model:
- Two named speakers.
- Conversational rhythm.
- One topic or situation guides the episode.
- The speakers explain through dialogue, not through a detached article voice.
- Light humor is allowed when it comes from the speakers.
- The episode ends with a brief listener-facing close.

Voice and style:
- Friendly spoken Spanish.
- Natural pauses, reactions, small jokes, and follow-up questions.
- The dialogue should sound like two people helping the learner understand a topic.
- Avoid turning the conversation into a list of tips.
- Keep the educational support inside the flow.

Core structure:
- H1 title.
- Short English intro that explains the topic and listening goal.
- Main podcast conversation.
- Selected vocabulary from the conversation.
- Useful phrases from the conversation.
- Practice section.
- Roleplay prompts.
- Discussion questions.

Dialogue rules:
- Use speaker labels on every line.
- Keep two speakers throughout the main conversation.
- Use enough turns for the exchange to develop.
- Add English support only after selected lines or phrases that help comprehension.
- Do not translate every line.

## street_interview

Use for lesson entries that feel like a street interview with several people.

Editorial model:
- One interviewer.
- Several interviewees.
- Each interviewee gets a small block with a distinct voice, routine, preference, or situation.
- The format should feel like the interviewer is moving through a public place and asking people a shared question.

Voice and style:
- Spoken Spanish with short exchanges.
- Each interviewee should sound different.
- The interviewer keeps the lesson organized with simple questions.
- The tone may include small corrections, clarification questions, or human details.

Core structure:
- H1 title.
- Short English intro that explains the street-interview situation.
- Useful vocabulary before the interviews.
- Interview blocks by person, couple, group, or role.
- Selected English support after useful questions or answers.
- Useful phrases from the interviews.
- Practice section.
- Roleplay prompts.
- Discussion questions.

Interview rules:
- Use one interviewer across the lesson.
- Use at least three interview blocks when the topic supports it.
- Give each block a heading.
- Keep answers long enough to feel human, not like examples in isolation.
- Add English support only for selected lines, phrases, or vocabulary.
- Do not translate every line.
